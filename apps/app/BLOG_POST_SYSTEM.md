### 3. Opening Hook Structure (100-150 words)

**Formula:**
1. **Problem Statement** (1-2 sentences)
2. **Empathy Connection** (1 sentence)
3. **Solution Preview** (1-2 sentences)
4. **Credibility Signal** (1 sentence)

**Example:**
> "Exam stress affects 9 out of 10 students, often leading to blanked minds and poor performance despite months of preparation. If you've ever sat in an exam room feeling your heart race while your carefully memorized facts seem to vanish, you're not alone. ### 4. Content Body Structure

#### A. Overview/Introduction Section
- **Purpose**: Set context and expectations
- **Length**: 50-100 words
- **Format**: Bullet points or numbered list showing what readers will learn

#### B. Main Content Sections (3-7 sections max)

**Section Structure Template:**
```markdown
## [Number]. [Descriptive Heading with Benefit]

### Quick Overview
- [2-3 bullet points summarizing the section]

### The Problem/Why This Matters
[1-2 paragraphs explaining the issue/importance]

### The Solution/Method
[Step-by-step instructions or detailed explanation]

### Real Example
[Concrete example showing the method in action]

### Pro Tips
- [3-5 actionable tips in bullet format]

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- [2-3 mistakes students commonly make]
```

#### C. Visual Elements Requirements

**Per Section Include:**
- 1 relevant image (with alt text)
- 1-2 callout boxes/highlights
- 1 list or table (when applicable)

**Visual Content Types:**
- Screenshots of study tools
- Infographics summarizing key points
- Progress charts/timelines
- Before/after comparisons
- Student workspace photos

### 1. Evergreen vs. Timely Content

#### Evergreen Content (70% of posts)
- Study techniques
- Exam strategies
- Subject fundamentals
- Time management skills

#### Timely Content (30% of posts)
- Exam board updates

### 2. Content Series Development

#### Series Structure
- **Introduction Post**: Overview of the series
- **Core Posts**: 3-7 detailed posts on subtopics
- **Summary Post**: Comprehensive guide combining all parts
- **Action Post**: Implementation guide

#### Example Series: "GCSE Maths Mastery"
1. "Complete GCSE Maths Study Plan (Introduction)"
2. "Mastering Algebra: Step-by-Step Guide"
3. "Geometry Made Simple: Visual Learning Techniques"
4. "Statistics & Probability: Real-World Applications"
5. "GCSE Maths Formula Sheet (Complete Guide)"
6. "30-Day GCSE Maths Revision Plan (Action Guide)"

### 3. Content Personalization

#### Student Level Adaptation
- **Struggling Students**: More basic explanations, confidence building
- **Average Students**: Balanced approach, skill building
- **High Achievers**: Advanced techniques, challenge content

#### Subject-Specific Customization
- **STEM Subjects**: Formula integration, problem-solving focus
- **Humanities**: Essay writing, analysis techniques
- **Languages**: Practice exercises, cultural context

### 4. Multi-Format Content

#### Primary Formats
- **Ultimate Guides**: 3,000+ word comprehensive posts

- **Case Studies**: Student success story analysis
- **Resource Roundups**: Tool and app recommendations

#### Supporting Formats
- **Checklists**: PDF downloads

- **Worksheets**: Practice exercises
- **Infographics**: Visual summaries

## ðŸŽ¨ Brand Voice and Tone Guidelines

### Voice Characteristics
- **Expert but Approachable**: Knowledgeable without being intimidating
- **Encouraging**: Positive and supportive language
- **Practical**: Focus on actionable advice
- **Student-Centered**: Written from student perspective

### Tone Variations by Content Type
- **Study Guides**: Professional, structured, comprehensive
- **Exam Tips**: Encouraging, urgent, confident
- **Stress Management**: Calm, understanding, supportive
- **Success Stories**: Inspiring, motivational, relatable

### Language Guidelines
- Use "you" to create connection
- Avoid jargon unless explained
- Include specific examples
- Balance confidence with humility
- Acknowledge student struggles
