# Mark Allocation Research - UK Exam Boards

Comprehensive research on real exam mark allocations across AQA, Edexcel, and OCR for GCSE and A-Level subjects.

## AQA GCSE Mark Allocations

### Mathematics
- **Paper Structure**: 3 papers, 80 marks each (240 total)
- **Mark Types**: M1, M2 (method marks), A1, A2 (accuracy marks), B1 (independent marks)
- **Typical Ranges**:
  - 1-2 marks: Simple calculations, multiple choice
  - 3-4 marks: Multi-step problems
  - 5-6 marks: Complex problem solving, extended reasoning

### Physics
- **Paper Structure**: 70 marks total per paper
- **Question Types**:
  - 1 mark: Simple recall, definitions
  - 2-3 marks: Basic calculations with substitution
  - 4-6 marks: Multi-step calculations ("Six marker" calculations)
  - 6 marks: Extended response (level-based marking)

### Chemistry
- **Mark Types**: M marks (method), A marks (accuracy), ECF (error carried forward)
- **Question Ranges**:
  - 1-2 marks: Basic knowledge, simple calculations
  - 3-4 marks: Explanations, multi-step calculations
  - 6 marks: Extended response (QWC - Quality of Written Communication)

### Biology
- **Command Words**: Describe (AO1), Explain (AO2), Compare, Evaluate (AO3), Suggest
- **Mark Ranges**:
  - 1 mark: Simple recall, naming
  - 2-3 marks: Explanations, comparisons
  - 6 marks: Extended response using BUG technique (Box, Underline, Glance)

### English Literature
- **Paper Structure**: Two papers
- **Mark Distribution for 30-mark questions**:
  - AO1: 12 marks (analysis and interpretation)
  - AO2: 12 marks (language, structure, form)
  - AO3: 6 marks (context)
  - AO4: 4 marks (technical accuracy/SPAG)
- **Grade Boundaries**: Grade 9 ≈ 26.4/30 marks

### History
- **Essay Questions**:
  - 16 marks: Content analysis
  - 20 marks: 16 marks content + 4 marks SPAG
  - Structure: PEEL (Point, Evidence, Explain, Link)

## AQA A-Level Mark Allocations

### Mathematics
- **Paper Structure**: 100 marks per paper
- **Mark Types**: M (method), A (accuracy), B (basic), SC (special case)
- **Question Ranges**: 1-15+ marks depending on complexity

### Physics
- **Mark Principles**: ECF (Error Carry Forward), significant figures assessment
- **Ranges**:
  - 1-3 marks: Simple calculations, definitions
  - 4-6 marks: Multi-step problems
  - 7-15 marks: Complex problem-solving, extended calculations

### English Literature
- **Essay Structure**: 25 marks per essay question
- **Assessment Objectives**:
  - AO1: 28% weighting
  - AO2 & AO3: 24% each
  - AO4 & AO5: 12% each
- **Paper Structure**: 75 marks total, 2.5 hours

### History
- **Essay Questions**:
  - 25 marks: Standard A-Level essays
  - 30 marks: Compulsory interpretation questions (Section A)
- **Assessment Focus**: AO1 (knowledge), AO2 (analysis), AO3 (interpretation)

## Edexcel GCSE Mark Allocations

- **Grade Distribution**: Higher tier - 50% targeting grades 4-6, 50% targeting grades 7-9
- **Paper Structure**: 3 papers similar to AQA

### Physics

- **Question Types**:
  - 1-3 marks: Basic recall, simple calculations
  - 6 marks: Extended response (level-based: 0, 1-2, 3-4, 5-6 marks)

### English Literature

  - AO1: 16 marks

- **20-mark questions**: AO2 only (Section A)

### History
- **Standard Format**: 16 marks + 4 SPAG = 20 total
- **Question Style**: "How far do you agree?" essays
- **Total Assessment**: 160 marks + 8 SPAG across all papers

## Key Patterns Identified

### Low Mark Questions (1-3 marks)
- Simple recall, definitions
- Basic calculations with single steps
- Multiple choice questions

### Medium Mark Questions (4-6 marks)
- Multi-step calculations with partial credit
- Explanations requiring multiple points
- Short extended responses

### High Mark Questions (15-30 marks)
- Extended essays with level-based marking
- Complex problem-solving across multiple topics
- Comparative analysis requiring sustained argument

### SPAG (Spelling, Punctuation, Grammar)
- Typically 4 marks additional to content marks
- Applied to specific extended response questions
- Required across all exam boards for extended writing

## Subject-Specific Patterns

### Mathematics & Sciences
- Heavy emphasis on method marks (M marks)
- Error carry forward to prevent double penalty
- Ranges typically 1-6 marks per question part

### Essay Subjects (English, History)
- 15-30 mark essays standard
- Level-based marking with multiple assessment objectives
- SPAG marks additional to content assessment

### Mixed Subjects
- Combination of short answer (1-4 marks) and extended response (6+ marks)
- Balance between knowledge recall and analytical skills

## Additional Subject Research

### Computer Science

#### AQA GCSE/A-Level Computer Science
- **Programming Questions**: 1-9 marks per component (class creation, inheritance, method overrides)
- **Algorithm Questions**: Typically 3-5 marks for algorithm design/implementation
- **Mark Types**: Individual programming skills awarded separately
- **Assessment Approach**: Syntactically correct code required for full marks at A-Level
- **Design Marks**: Available even for non-working code if appropriate techniques selected

### Economics

#### AQA A-Level Economics
- **15-mark questions**: Analysis questions (no evaluation required)
  - Structure: 2-3 substantial analytical points
  - Timing: 15-20 minutes
- **25-mark questions**: Essay questions with evaluation
  - Structure: Minimum 6 points (half explanation, half argumentative)
  - Requires: Application, analysis, and evaluation

#### Edexcel A-Level Economics  
- **15-mark questions**: Limited points evaluated in depth
- **25-mark questions**: 2-3 major analytical points preferred over many superficial points
- **Assessment Focus**: Context integration essential for top marks

### Business Studies

#### GCSE Business (AQA/Edexcel)
- **6-mark questions**: Analysis/discussion without evaluation
- **9-mark questions**: 5 linked development strands for full marks
- **12-mark questions**: Application (AO2) and Evaluation (AO3b) often limiting factors
- **Structure**: Definition (2 marks) + BLT chains (Because, Leading to, Therefore)
- **Paper Changes**: From 2023 - separate source booklets, 1h 45min timing

#### A-Level Business
- **Case Study Analysis**: Heavy emphasis on application to business context
- **Assessment Objectives**: Knowledge, Application, Analysis, Evaluation
- **Answer Structure**: Specific application to case study essential

### Psychology

#### AQA A-Level Psychology
- **16-mark questions**: 
  - Standard essays: 6 marks AO1, 10 marks AO3
  - Context essays: 6 marks AO1, 6 marks AO3, 4 marks AO2
  - Timing: 20-25 minutes
- **12-mark questions** (AS Level): 6 marks knowledge, 6 marks evaluation
- **8-mark questions**: 3:5 skills balance (3 marks AO1, 5 marks AO3)
- **Research Studies**: No specific number required, sufficient for coherent discussion
- **Study Details**: Key statistics helpful, dates not essential unless specified

### Geography

#### AQA GCSE Geography
- **Fieldwork Assessment**: 
  - Section B Paper 3: 12 marks AO4, 3 marks AO3 (unfamiliar)
  - Question 5: Own fieldwork, predominantly AO3
  - Total: 15% of qualification
- **Typical Questions**: 3, 6, 9 mark questions common

#### AQA A-Level Geography
- **9-mark questions**: Include 3 marks SPaG assessment
- **20-mark questions**: Synoptic essay responses
- **Timing**: 12-13 minutes per 9-mark question (300-400 words)

#### Edexcel Geography
- **8-mark questions**: Extended response (final questions in sections)
- **12-mark questions**: Section C Paper 3 final questions
- **Question Range**: 1-12 marks across papers

## Cross-Board Patterns by Subject Type

### STEM Subjects (Maths, Sciences, Computer Science)
- **Low marks (1-3)**: Basic calculations, recall, definitions
- **Medium marks (4-6)**: Multi-step problems, method + accuracy
- **High marks (6+)**: Complex problem solving, extended reasoning
- **Error handling**: Follow-through marking prevents double penalty

### Essay Subjects (English, History, Economics)
- **Standard essays**: 15-30 marks
- **Level-based marking**: Multiple assessment objectives
- **SPAG**: Additional 4 marks for extended responses
- **Time allocation**: 20-25 minutes for 25-30 mark essays

### Applied Subjects (Business, Geography, Psychology)
- **Context application**: Essential for top marks
- **Case study analysis**: 6-20 marks depending on level
- **Fieldwork/Research**: Specific assessment criteria
- **Mixed assessment**: Combination of knowledge and application

### Practical Subjects (Computer Science)
- **Component-based marking**: Individual skills assessed separately
- **Syntax requirements**: Higher standards at A-Level
- **Problem-solving**: Emphasis on appropriate technique selection

## Complete Subject Analysis by Platform Support

### Combined Science (GCSE Only)

#### AQA Combined Science: Trilogy & Synergy
- **Paper Structure**: 6 papers total (Biology 1&2, Chemistry 1&2, Physics 1&2)
- **Foundation/Higher**: Both tiers available

  - Level 1 (1-2 marks): Basic knowledge

- **Papers 1-3**: Biology 1, Chemistry 1, Physics 1
- **Papers 4-6**: Biology 2, Chemistry 2, Physics 2
- **Grade Boundaries**: Typically slightly higher than AQA

#### OCR Combined Science
- **Similar Structure**: Foundation and Higher tiers
- **Grade Boundaries**: Generally similar to Edexcel, varies yearly

### Further Mathematics

#### AQA Further Mathematics (Level 2 Certificate 8365)
- **Difficulty Level**: Roughly equivalent to AS-level maths
- **Target Audience**: Top ~5% of mathematics students
- **Mark Types**: M (method), A (accuracy), B (basic)
- **Question Range**: 1-10+ marks per question
- **Paper Total**: Typically 80 marks

#### Edexcel A-Level Further Mathematics
- **Mark Categories**: M, A, B marks (same as standard A-Level Maths)
- **Method Marking**: 'Knowing a method and attempting to apply it'
- **Follow-through**: Credit for correct working even with early errors
- **Question Distribution**: Higher complexity than standard A-Level

## Science Subject 6-Mark QWC Questions - Universal Patterns

### Assessment Requirements (All Science Subjects)
- **Dual Assessment**: Both science content AND written communication
- **Time Allocation**: ~1 minute per mark (6-8 minutes for 6-mark questions)
- **Level Descriptors**: 3-level system (1-2, 3-4, 5-6 marks)
- **Answer Format**: Can use bullet points, diagrams, flow charts (not just prose)

### Command Words for 6-Mark Questions
- **Explain**: Chain of reasoning required
- **Compare**: Similarities and differences
- **Evaluate**: Judgments with justification
- **Analyze**: Break down and examine components

### Quality of Written Communication Criteria
- **Level 3 Requirements**:
  - Information presented coherently and logically
  - Excellent spelling, punctuation, grammar
  - Sound knowledge across wide range of mark scheme points
  - Appropriate use of specialist terminology

## Subject-Specific Patterns Summary

### STEM Core Subjects
- **Mathematics**: 1-15+ marks, M/A/B marking, follow-through provisions
- **Physics**: 1-15+ marks, ECF marking, 6-mark extended responses
- **Chemistry**: 1-6+ marks, QWC questions, calculation and explanation mix
- **Biology**: 1-6+ marks, QWC questions, limited calculations compared to other sciences
- **Combined Science**: Same patterns as individual sciences, 6-mark QWC emphasis
- **Further Mathematics**: 1-10+ marks, AS-level equivalent difficulty
- **Computer Science**: 1-9 marks per component, syntax requirements

### Essay and Analysis Subjects  
- **English Literature**: 25-30 marks (A-Level), 30 marks + 4 SPAG (GCSE)
- **History**: 16+4 SPAG (GCSE), 25-30 marks (A-Level)
- **Economics**: 15-25 marks, context application essential
- **Business**: 6-12 marks (GCSE), case study application required
- **Psychology**: 8-16 marks, research study integration required
- **Geography**: 3-20 marks, fieldwork and case study emphasis

## Final Cross-Board Verification

### Consistency Across Exam Boards
- **6-mark QWC**: Universal in all science subjects across AQA/Edexcel/OCR
- **Essay marking**: 15-30 marks consistent across all boards for extended responses  
- **SPAG**: 4 marks additional for extended writing (all boards)
- **Method marking**: M/A/B system consistent across mathematical subjects
- **Level descriptors**: 3-6 level systems standard for extended responses
